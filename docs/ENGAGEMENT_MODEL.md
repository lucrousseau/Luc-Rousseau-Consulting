# Premium Engagement Model — Senior Product Engineer

## Guiding principles

- **No** hourly work, isolated feature dev, or pure execution tickets.
- **Yes** strategic involvement, product-level impact, architecture oversight, high-leverage contribution, recurring retainer structure.

---

## 1. Three structured engagement models

### Model A — **Technical Product Steward** (Retainer)

**Structure:** Fixed monthly retainer (e.g. 2–4 days/month), 3–6 month minimum. Recurring cadence: architecture review, backlog refinement, technical decisions, and hands-on work on the highest-leverage items.

**What you get:**

- A dedicated senior who owns the _technical direction_ of the product, not just delivery.
- Regular architecture and roadmap alignment (e.g. bi-weekly or monthly).
- Backlog structuring, dependency mapping, and prioritisation that reflects both product goals and technical reality.
- Targeted implementation on critical path items (APIs, platform foundations, debt reduction, performance).
- One clear voice for “how we build” and “what we build next” from a technical lens.

**Ideal for:** Product-led teams that have (or want) a strong PM but lack a senior technical owner; scale-ups with a growing codebase and need for clarity and consistency.

---

### Model B — **Product & System Architect** (Project-based retainer)

**Structure:** Phased engagement with a fixed scope per phase (e.g. “Phase 1: Architecture clarity and roadmap,” “Phase 2: Foundation and API design,” “Phase 3: Handover and governance”). Each phase is a retainer (e.g. 1–3 months) with clear outcomes and optional follow-on retainers for execution or oversight.

**What you get:**

- A structured path from “unclear or messy” to “clear architecture and plan.”
- Deliverables: architecture doc, technical roadmap, API contracts, backlog structure, and optionally implementation of core pieces.
- No open-ended “dev for hire”—each phase has defined outputs and success criteria.
- Option to continue with a lighter retainer (Model A) for ongoing oversight after the heavy lift.

**Ideal for:** Teams about to invest in a rebuild, platformisation, or headless/multi-site; organisations that need to reduce technical debt before scaling.

---

### Model C — **Fractional Product Engineer** (Dedicated retainer)

**Structure:** Higher commitment retainer (e.g. 8–12 days/month), 6–12 month minimum. Luc acts as your part-time senior Product Engineer: strategy, architecture, and execution in one role, with a consistent presence (e.g. 2–3 days/week).

**What you get:**

- One person who combines product-level thinking with hands-on technical delivery.
- Continuous alignment: roadmap, architecture, and backlog stay in sync with business goals.
- No handoff between “someone who decides” and “someone who builds”—decisions and implementation live in the same head.
- Best fit when you want a senior technical partner without a full-time hire, or while you ramp an internal team.

**Ideal for:** Startups or scale-ups that need a single high-leverage technical leader; teams that have been burned by “strategy without execution” or “execution without strategy.”

## 3. Expected business outcomes

Across all models, clients can expect:

- **Faster, safer decisions** — Technical choices (stack, APIs, build vs buy, debt paydown) are made with product and business context, not in isolation.
- **Less rework** — Architecture and roadmap are aligned early; fewer “we built the wrong thing” or “we have to redo it” moments.
- **Clearer roadmap** — Backlog and dependencies reflect both product priorities and technical reality; stakeholders see a coherent plan.
- **Scalable foundations** — Multi-site, headless, performance, and CI/CD are treated as part of the product proposition, not afterthoughts.
- **Reduced risk** — Fewer surprises from legacy or tangled systems; technical debt is acknowledged and prioritised instead of ignored or blindly rewritten.

**By model:**

- **A:** Sustained alignment and quality over time; technical direction that doesn’t drift.
- **B:** A clear “before and after”; a foundation that supports the next 12–24 months of product work.
- **C:** A single accountable technical leader; product and eng moving in the same direction with minimal coordination overhead.

## 4. How this differs from hiring a senior developer

| Dimension          | Senior developer (hire or contractor)           | Product Engineer engagement (Luc)                                                                            |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Scope**          | Tasks, features, projects as defined by others. | Ownership of technical direction, architecture, and how work is prioritised and structured.                  |
| **Output**         | Code, reviews, implementation.                  | Code _plus_ strategy: roadmap, architecture, backlog structure, and decisions that affect the whole product. |
| **Engagement**     | Often hourly or project-based on a given spec.  | Retainer-based; strategic involvement and high-leverage work by design.                                      |
| **Responsibility** | Delivers what’s asked.                          | Challenges what’s asked when it doesn’t serve the product or the system; proposes better paths.              |
| **Alignment**      | Works from requirements and tickets.            | Ensures requirements and tickets align with product goals and technical sustainability.                      |

**In short:** A senior developer executes a plan. A Product Engineer in this model helps _define_ the plan, owns the technical side of it, and executes the highest-leverage parts of it. You’re not buying “senior hands”—you’re buying **strategic technical ownership** and **high-leverage contribution** within a recurring relationship.
