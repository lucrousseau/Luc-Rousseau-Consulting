# Product Engineer offer — 4 pillars

Tone: Senior, strategic, outcome-driven.

---

## Pillar 1 — Product & roadmap clarity

**Short title:** Product & roadmap clarity

**Explanation (2–3 sentences):**  
I work with you to turn product goals and stakeholder expectations into a clear, sequenced plan. Priorities are explicit, dependencies are mapped, and the technical roadmap is aligned with what the business needs to ship. No more “everyone has a different view of what we’re building”—one coherent story that product, engineering, and leadership can follow.

**Concrete outcomes:**

- A technical roadmap tied to product milestones and business outcomes
- Backlog structured by impact, dependency, and risk (not just “urgent”)
- Shared language between product and engineering on scope, sequence, and success criteria
- Fewer surprises in planning and fewer “we didn’t know that was in scope” moments

**Problems it solves:**

- Roadmap and backlog feel chaotic or driven by whoever shouts loudest
- Product and engineering disagree on what “done” or “next” means
- Stakeholders lack a single view of what’s being built and when
- Hard to say “no” or “not yet” because there’s no clear prioritisation framework

---

## Pillar 2 — Architecture & system design

**Short title:** Architecture & system design

**Explanation (2–3 sentences):**  
I define or clarify how your system is structured: boundaries between services or modules, APIs and contracts, data flow, and how the stack can evolve without breaking things. The goal is an architecture that supports the product today and can scale with clear, intentional choices—not accidental complexity or “we’ll fix it later.”

**Concrete outcomes:**

- Documented architecture (or an updated one) that reflects how the system actually works and how it should evolve
- Clear API and integration contracts so front-end, back-end, and third parties know their boundaries
- Decisions on build vs buy, stack consolidation, and headless/platform approach that are justified and communicable
- A shared mental model so new work fits the system instead of working around it

**Problems it solves:**

- “Nobody really knows how it all fits together” or “we’re scared to change X”
- Sprawl: many ad-hoc integrations, duplicated logic, or inconsistent patterns
- Big rewrites or “we need to redo the foundation” with no clear path
- Headless, multi-site, or platform decisions feel like guesswork

---

## Pillar 3 — Technical debt reduction

**Short title:** Technical debt reduction

**Explanation (2–3 sentences):**  
Technical debt is treated as a product and risk concern, not a side project. I help you identify what matters most—security, performance, maintainability, or scalability—and prioritise paydown in a way that supports delivery instead of blocking it. We reduce risk and cost by tackling the right debt in the right order, with clear criteria for “good enough” so the team can ship while improving.

**Concrete outcomes:**

- A prioritised debt backlog (what to fix, in what order, and why) aligned with product and risk
- Incremental improvement: no “stop the world” rewrites unless justified; steady, measurable progress
- Clear ownership and criteria so debt doesn’t silently grow or get ignored
- Fewer production incidents and rework caused by unknown or neglected debt

**Problems it solves:**

- Debt is either ignored (“we’ll fix it later”) or triggers panic rewrites
- Team spends more time fighting the codebase than building features
- Hard to estimate or deliver because of hidden dependencies and fragile areas
- Security, performance, or reliability issues keep resurfacing

---

## Pillar 4 — Delivery & execution supervision

**Short title:** Delivery & execution supervision

**Explanation (2–3 sentences):**  
I provide senior oversight of how work gets done: quality bar, release discipline, and whether what’s built matches the architecture and roadmap. I don’t replace your team—I ensure standards are clear, reviews are meaningful, and delivery stays aligned with the plan. When I write code, it’s on the highest-leverage items; the rest of the time I’m making sure the system and the process support sustainable, predictable delivery.

**Concrete outcomes:**

- Consistent quality and alignment: what ships matches the agreed architecture and product intent
- Clear definition of “done” (tests, docs, security, performance) so nothing slips through
- CI/CD and release practices that support safe, repeatable deployments
- A single accountable voice for “how we build” so the team can focus on building

**Problems it solves:**

- Quality and consistency vary; “it works on my machine” or “we’ll clean it up later” is the norm
- Releases are risky or manual; no clear process for what gets reviewed and how
- Gap between “what we decided” and “what we built”; architecture and roadmap drift
- Team lacks a senior technical reference for standards and trade-offs
